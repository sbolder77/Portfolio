Secure Software Development

Learning Outcomes

- Acquire a critical understanding of the concept of abstraction in programming.
- Develop an understanding of the basic principles of secure development methodologies.
- Explore how to undertake analysis, program design, software construction and testing required for software development.
- Demonstrate an understanding of the basic principles of architecture, as well as traditional and contemporary Software Development Life Cycle (SDLC) models, such as TOGAF and Agile.
- Learn in a team environment, demonstratE how to deal with conflicts and how to make compromises, and be able to critically evaluate the effectiveness of the approaches.
- Utilise the opportunity to reflect on and evaluate your own personal development.

Formative Activities

The module covered a range of formative assignments including coding concepts and seminar activities to support the objectives and delivery of a team development assignment. The content covered a wide range of concepts all of which were developed through the Codio IDE or independently in Visual Studio. Most of the evaluation and prototyping was carried out in Visual Studio to utilise a more familiar development environment.

Collaborative Discussions

The module consisted of 2 collaborative discussions. We were asked to submit to student forums to analyse and discuss each others posts on 2 subjects.

Unit 1-3 - UML Flowchart

We were asked to select one of the coding weaknesses which have been identified by OWASP and create a flowchart for a piece of software and the steps which may lead to the weakness occurring. We discussed which UML models might be used and why are they the most appropriate choice(s)?

  • View Discussion 1

Unit 8-10 - TrueCrypt

TrueCrypt was a popular and well-respected operating system add-on that could create encrypted volumes on a Windows and/or Linux system. We were asked to discuss it's weaknesses and it's relevance and if it would be a suitable tool to recommend it to a friend.
